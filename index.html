Analytical Model Reference

This engineering tool analyses atmospheric turbulence effects and adaptive optics performance for free-space optical communications (FSOC). The core logic dynamically converts between seeing conditions and the Fried parameter (r₀), computing the exact D/r₀ ratio for a specified telescope aperture.

η ≈ η_max * exp(-½ C_J (D/r₀)^(5/3))

α = 0.455 · (D/r₀)^(5/6) · (λ/D)

Literature Validation: Coupling efficiency derivations strictly align with theoretical and experimental data from Wang et al. (2018), "Performance analysis of an adaptive optics system for free-space optics communication through atmospheric turbulence" (Scientific Reports).

The 1-axis tip-tilt RMS angular deviation (α) formulation utilises the standard G-tilt variance coefficient for centroid tracking through Kolmogorov turbulence.

Key Parameters:

  • Wavelength (λ): Typically 1550nm for European telecom standards, 808nm for wavefront sensing.
  • Seeing: Atmospheric turbulence measured in arcseconds, strictly anchored to a 500 nm reference wavelength.
  • r₀: Fried parameter defining the coherence length, calculated dynamically at the operational wavelength (λ) using standard λ^(6/5) scaling.
  • D: Telescope aperture diameter.
  • J: Quantity of corrected Zernike polynomials.
  • η_max: Maximum coupling efficiency (accounts for telescope central obscuration and fibre NA limits).
